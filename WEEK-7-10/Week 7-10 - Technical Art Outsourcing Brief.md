## Project Overview

In this collaborative project, you will operate as a **Technical Art Outsourcing Studio**, providing specialized technical art services to Games Design students who will act as your **clients**. This simulates real-world industry workflows where external studios are contracted to deliver specific technical art assets for game projects.

You will select asset requests from a shared spreadsheet, communicate with clients to clarify requirements, and deliver professional-quality technical art assets within agreed timelines.

---

## Brief

### Your Role: Technical Art Outsourcing Studio

You are a technical artist working for an outsourcing studio that specializes in:
- VFX systems (Niagara/Cascade)
- Advanced materials and shaders
- Animation systems and rigging
- Procedural systems and tools
- Technical problem-solving

**Games Design students** (your clients) have submitted asset requests via a shared spreadsheet. Your job is to:
1. Review available requests
2. Select 2-5 requests that match your skills and interests (based on complexity and your capacity)
3. Communicate with the client to clarify requirements
4. Deliver professional-quality assets with documentation
5. Iterate based on client feedback


---

### Handover Documentation
**Required Documentation: (1 For Each asset or collection of assets)**

#### Cover Page
- Asset name and type
- Your name and contact
- Client name and project
- Delivery date
- Version number

#### Asset Overview
- Brief description of what the asset does
- Key features and capabilities


#### Integration Guide
- Step-by-step instructions for importing into client's project
- Required dependencies or plugins (if applicable)
- Setup instructions with screenshots
- Parameter explanations (exposed variables, material instances, etc.)
- Usage examples

#### Customization Guide
- How to modify colors, scale, intensity, etc.
- Exposed parameters and their effects
- Tips for adapting the asset to different use cases
- Troubleshooting common issues

#### Technical Documentation
- Material graph breakdowns (for shaders)
- Niagara system structure (for VFX)
- Blueprint logic explanations (for systems)
